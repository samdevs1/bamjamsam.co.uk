STAND OUT

What makes me stand out is my broad wealth of experience in a range of different environments. I established communication skills through PALS part time whilst at university, standing up and facilitating peer learning within a classroom environment. I’ve built relationships and learnt from the Fijian whilst volunteering abroad for GVI. All these skills make me a valuable candidate that I feel will offer a wealth of experience that truly aligns with the needs outlined for the graduate trainee role.

TEAMWORK

My role within a team is often mediator. A mediator is someone who get everyone involved. I try to facilitate discussion, interjecting my own thoughts when necessary. Mediator allows me to adapt to a leadership or subordinate role depending on the project, tasks, and needs of the group. This is a skill I’ve applied as Vice president of the Underwater Hockey Society and throughout my degree most recently as part of my Integrating Project module where I led five team members.

DIFFICULT SITUATION

Led five members as part of my Integrating Project module. With weekly meetings I would assign tasks, have my team feedback what they've done in regard to the project and if any problems arose how could we deal with them together. During this experience I calculated my approach to help a team member who was considering leaving university in a neutral non-judgmental manner. Employing active listening skills and asking appropriate questions to get to the root of the problem, regularly re-prioritising my schedule to support him whilst coming to a decision to withdraw from university. Throughout this issue and especially following his departure from the project I continued to motivate the team members whilst working towards passing the substantial project.

ABOUT ME

My personal profile.

My name is Sam Watson and I am an undergraduate interested in taking a year out as part of my degree, putting classroom theory into the real-world environment and experiencing all aspects of business. I am in my penultimate year of study with a successful background working independently and collaboratively with academics as part of the Peer Assisted Learning Scheme (PALS). PALS facilitates regular study sessions for students, by students. With an ever adapting technology industry I am constantly learning new techniques and processes putting this to practice. With three years of experience in software development for desktop and the web I've progressed from analysis to design and everything in between.
